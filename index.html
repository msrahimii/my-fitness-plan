Weekly Training Plan

Day 1: Full Body A (Dumbbell Focus)

Focus on controlling the weight and feeling the muscles work. This builds your foundation.

Warm-up (10 min)

  • Treadmill: 5-7 minutes of light walking, progressing to a light jog.
  • Dynamic Stretches: Arm circles, leg swings, torso twists (30 seconds each).

Goblet Squat

Sets: 3 Reps: 10-12

Equipment: 1 Dumbbell

Starting Weight: 10-12 kg

Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

Form: Hold one dumbbell vertically against your chest. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Push your hips back and bend your knees as if sitting in a chair. Go as deep as you can while keeping your chest up and back straight. Push through your heels to return to the start.

[Image of a Dumbbell Goblet Squat]

Why: This is a fantastic compound exercise for your entire lower body (quads, glutes, hamstrings) and core.

Dumbbell Bench Press

Sets: 3 Reps: 10-12

Equipment: 2 Dumbbells, Bench (if no bench, do a floor press)

Starting Weight: 8-10 kg per dumbbell

Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

Form: Lie on a flat bench holding a dumbbell in each hand above your chest, palms facing forward. Lower the dumbbells slowly to the sides of your chest. Pause, then press them back up to the starting position.

[Image of a Dumbbell Bench Press]

Why: Builds your chest (pectorals), shoulders (deltoids), and triceps.

Single Arm Dumbbell Row

Sets: 3 (per side) Reps: 10-12

Equipment: 1 Dumbbell, Bench

Starting Weight: 10-12 kg

Rest: 60 seconds between sets.

Form: Place one knee and the same-side hand on the bench. Keep your back flat. Hold the dumbbell in the opposite hand with your arm extended. Pull the dumbbell up towards your hip, squeezing your back muscle. Lower it slowly.

Why: Excellent for building a strong back (lats) and improving posture.

Plank

Sets: 3 Reps: 30-45 sec hold

Equipment: Bodyweight

Rest: 60 seconds between sets.

Form: Hold your body in a straight line from head to heels, supported on your forearms and toes. Brace your core and glutes. Do not let your hips sag.

[Image of a Plank]

Why: Builds core strength and stability, which is essential for all other lifts.

Cool-down (5-10 min)

  • Light Walk: 2-3 minutes on the treadmill.
  • Static Stretches: Hold stretches for your chest, back, and legs for 20-30 seconds each.

Day 2: Cardio (HIIT)

High-Intensity Interval Training is amazing for burning fat in a short amount of time. Push yourself in the "Run" intervals!

Treadmill HIIT Session (Total: 25-30 min)

  • Warm-up (5 min): Brisk walk (e.g., 5-6 kph).
  • HIIT Intervals (Repeat 10 times):
    • 30 seconds RUN: Find a challenging pace (e.g., 8-10 kph). You should be breathing hard.
    • 60 seconds WALK: Slow down to a recovery walk (e.g., 4-5 kph).
  • Cool-down (5-10 min): Slow walk, bringing your heart rate down.

Day 3: Full Body B (Hoist Mi6 Focus)

Today we use the functional trainer. The cables provide constant tension. Remember, the weight on the stack is not the same as a dumbbell. Start light!

Warm-up (10 min)

  • Treadmill: 5-7 minutes of light walking/jogging.
  • Dynamic Stretches: Arm circles, leg swings, torso twists.

Cable Chest Press

Sets: 3 Reps: 10-12

Equipment: Hoist Mi6 (Dual Pulleys)

Starting Weight: 20-30 lbs *on each stack*.

Rest: 60-90 seconds.

Form: Set both pulleys to chest height. Grab the handles and step forward into a staggered stance. With your chest up, press the handles forward and together until your arms are straight. Squeeze your chest. Slowly return to the start.

Why: Builds the chest and shoulders with constant tension from the cables.

Seated Cable Row

Sets: 3 Reps: 10-12

Equipment: Hoist Mi6, Bench (if possible)

Starting Weight: 30-40 lbs *on each stack* (if using both) or 40-50 lbs total.

Rest: 60-90 seconds.

Form: Sit with your back straight. Grab the handles with arms extended. Pull the handles towards your lower ribs, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Keep your chest up. Slowly return to the start.

Why: Builds the mid-back (lats, rhomboids) for thickness and posture.

Cable Bicep Curl

Sets: 3 Reps: 12-15

Equipment: Hoist Mi6 (Dual Pulleys, low)

Starting Weight: 10-15 lbs *on each stack*.

Rest: 60 seconds.

Form: Set both pulleys to the lowest setting. Grab the handles. Keeping your elbows pinned to your sides, curl the handles up towards your shoulders. Squeeze your biceps at the top, then slowly lower.

Why: Isolates the bicep muscles.

Cable Tricep Pushdown

Sets: 3 Reps: 12-15

Equipment: Hoist Mi6 (High pulley), Rope or Bar attachment

Starting Weight: 20-30 lbs *total stack weight*.

Rest: 60 seconds.

Form: Attach a rope or straight bar to a high pulley. Grab the attachment with palms facing down. Keep your elbows tucked into your sides. Push the bar down until your arms are fully extended. Squeeze your triceps. Slowly return.

Why: Isolates the tricep muscles (back of the arm).

Cable Crunch

Sets: 3 Reps: 15-20

Equipment: Hoist Mi6 (High pulley), Rope attachment

Starting Weight: 25-35 lbs *total stack weight*.

Rest: 60 seconds.

Form: Kneel facing the machine, holding the rope handles on either side of your head. Crunch forward, bringing your elbows towards your knees, rounding your back and squeezing your abs. Slowly return up.

Why: Adds resistance to your abdominal training.

Cool-down (5-10 min)

  • Light Walk: 2-3 minutes.
  • Static Stretches: Chest, back, arms, and abs.

Day 4: Cardio (LISS)

Low-Intensity Steady State. This is about building your aerobic base and burning calories without stressing your body too much. It's great for active recovery.

Treadmill LISS Session (Total: 40-50 min)

  • Warm-up (5 min): Slow walk (e.g., 4-5 kph).
  • Steady State (30-40 min):
    • Speed: 5.5 - 6.5 kph (a very brisk walk).
    • Incline: 3.0 - 5.0 %
    • The goal is to keep your heart rate elevated but be able to hold a conversation.
  • Cool-down (5 min): Slow walk, no incline.

Day 5: Full Body C (Hybrid)

We're mixing it up today, using everything. The Dumbbell Thruster is a big metabolic exercise‚Äîperfect for finishing the week strong!

Warm-up (10 min)

  • Treadmill: 5-7 minutes of light jogging.
  • Dynamic Stretches: Arm circles, leg swings, torso twists.

Dumbbell Lunge

Sets: 3 (per side) Reps: 10-12

Equipment: 2 Dumbbells

Starting Weight: 5-8 kg per dumbbell

Rest: 60 seconds.

Form: Hold a dumbbell in each hand. Step forward with one leg, lowering your hips until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle. Your front knee should be above your ankle, and your back knee should not touch the ground. Push off your front foot to return to the start. Alternate legs or do all reps on one side before switching.

[Image of a Dumbbell Lunge]

Why: Works the quads, glutes, and hamstrings one leg at a time, improving balance.

Lat Pulldown (Mi6)

Sets: 3 Reps: 10-12

Equipment: Hoist Mi6 (High Pulleys), Lat Bar attachment or Dual Handles

Starting Weight: 40-50 lbs *total stack weight*.

Rest: 60-90 seconds.

Form: Sit or kneel facing the machine. Grab the bar with a wide grip. Keeping your chest up, pull the bar down to your upper chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Slowly return the bar to the starting position.

Why: The "vertical pull" equivalent of a pull-up. Builds a wide back.

Dumbbell Thruster

Sets: 3 Reps: 10

Equipment: 2 Dumbbells

Starting Weight: 5-8 kg per dumbbell

Rest: 90 seconds (this one is tough!).

Form: Hold two dumbbells at your shoulders. Perform a full squat (like the Goblet Squat). As you stand up, use the momentum to press the dumbbells overhead. Lower the dumbbells back to your shoulders and immediately begin the next squat. It's one fluid motion.

[Image of a Dumbbell Thruster]

Why: A total-body metabolic exercise. It hits your legs, core, shoulders, and triceps, and sky-rockets your heart rate. Amazing for fat loss.

Russian Twist

Sets: 3 Reps: 15-20 (total)

Equipment: Bodyweight or 5kg Dumbbell

Rest: 60 seconds.

Form: Sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet slightly off the ground. Lean back to engage your core. Clasp your hands together (or hold the dumbbell) and twist your torso from side to side, touching your hands to the floor on each side.

Why: Works the obliques (side abs) and core rotation.

Cool-down (5-10 min)

  • Light Walk: 2-3 minutes.
  • Static Stretches: Full body.

How to Progress (Read This!)

This plan is your starting point. The key to results is Progressive Overload. This means gradually making your workouts harder over time. Here's how:

  • Weight: This is the simplest way. Once you can complete all 3 sets of an exercise at the top end of the rep range (e.g., 12 reps) with good form, increase the weight by the smallest amount possible (e.g., add 2.5kg or move to the next dumbbell). In the next workout, you might only get 8-10 reps, and that's perfect! Work your way back up to 12.
  • Reps: If you can't increase the weight, add one more rep to each set.
  • Sets: You can add a 4th set to an exercise.
  • Cardio: For HIIT, add 1-2 more intervals. For LISS, increase the incline (e.g., 5.0% to 6.0%) or the duration (e.g., 30 min to 35 min).

Listen to your body. "Good form" is more important than heavy weight, especially now.

The Other 23 Hours

Your 1 hour in the gym is only part of the equation. Your primary goal of fat loss is HEAVILY influenced by nutrition.

üçé Nutrition

You don't need a crazy diet. Start simple: Focus on eating whole foods. Prioritize protein (chicken, fish, eggs, legumes) at every meal. This will help you feel full and build muscle. Reduce sugary drinks and processed snacks. To lose weight, you must be in a slight calorie deficit.

üíß Hydration

Drink water. A lot of it. Aim for 2-3 liters per day, plus more during your workouts. Being hydrated boosts your metabolism, improves performance, and helps your body recover. Carry a water bottle with you everywhere.

üò¥ Rest & Recovery

Your muscles are built while you rest, not while you train. Aim for 7-8 hours of quality sleep per night. Your two rest days are just as important as your training days. This is when your body repairs and gets stronger.
